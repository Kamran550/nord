As a general rule, safety representatives must be elected in all undertakings. If the business has less than 10 employees, the employer and employees can agree on another arrangement. All employees must be counted, including permanent employees (full-time and part-time), temporary employees and hired employees. Such an agreement must be in writing and contain a description of how the safety work is to be carried out specifically. Examples of what the agreement must contain: necessary working environment measures, meeting point, how safety issues are raised, how risk conditions are handled, employer's duties, employees' duty to cooperate, etc.

The agreement period must be a maximum of two years and the agreement must contain who is the employees' representative or contact person. If the undertaking enters into an agreement on a different arrangement, all employees must sign the agreement. The employer must ensure that all employees have received the necessary training and information about the safety work. If the undertaking changes the number of employees so that they become 10 employees or more during the agreement period, a safety representative must be elected. The Norwegian Labor Inspection Authority can, by inspection and based on an assessment of risk conditions, order undertakings with less than 10 employees to elect a safety representative.

1. Training

The Working Environment Act § 3-5 requires the employer to undergo training in health, environmental and safety work. In addition, the employees shall have sufficient knowledge and skills in the systematic HSE work. We ensure that these provisions are followed up, and participate in relevant courses on health, environment and safety that are imposed on us. We also have routines for the implementation and control of training that requires certification.

2. Safety rounds

We conduct safety rounds at least twice a year. We use checklists from the HSE system. Findings from the safety round are used to assess the risk in a better way, and as input for new routines. Violations shall be recorded in the "Violation processing" module and corrected as described in the next point.

3. Violations and follow-up violations

Fails are registered as violations and they should be described how to resolve them, both in the short and long term. In addition, the person responsible for resolving the violation shall be registered, as well as a deadline with a reminder. All of the company's employees must participate actively in this work.

4. Accidents and injuries

We have our own forms for near misses and illnesses that may have their cause in conditions at the workplace. Serious accidents shall always be reported to the Norwegian Labor Inspection Authority, the nearest police authority and NAV. Forms, links and information can be found in the HSE system's form collection.

5. Risk analysis

We carry out risk analysis regularly. Both interpersonal relationships, material values ​​and the external environment will be areas that we have to survey in terms of risk. For us, risk assessment is a continuous way to think about safety, and is also carried out when changes have taken place that could have an impact.

6. Annual review

Every year we review our health, environment and safety work annually. The company's documentation is reviewed item by item. The purpose is to see if what is written corresponds with reality. Conditions that need to be corrected are recorded in the HSE system. The review is carried out each year by December.

7. Laws and regulations

We use https://lovdata.no/ and https://www.arbeidstilsynet.no/regelverk/om-regelverket/ to find updated laws and regulations. The general manager is responsible for and obtains the applicable laws and regulations.

Criteria for Assessment of Consequences and Probability

Consequences

1 Low significance Negligible personal injury/discomfort. No absence.
2 Less serious Minor, temporary personal injury. Absence of a maximum of three days.
3 Severe Serious injury/illness, long-term consequences. May result in longer absences.
4 Critical Permanent injuries/disability. Several individual cases of seriously injured/sick people.
5 Catastrophic Death. Many seriously ill/injured.

Probability

1 Very unlikely Probably won't happen. Never heard of.
2 Unlikely Probably won't happen. Have heard of.
3 Likely Will be able to happen one or more times during the time period.
4 Very likely Could happen several times a year.
5 Especially likely Will be able to happen weekly/daily.

Acceptance Criteria

10-25 High risk Not acceptable. Risk-reducing measures must be implemented.
5-9 Medium risk Risk-reducing measures are assessed (cost/benefit). Risk is monitored.
The combination of several unwanted events can have a reinforcing effect.
1-4 Low risk Accepted. Action not required.
